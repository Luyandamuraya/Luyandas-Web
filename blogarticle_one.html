Why CROSS JOINs Aren’t That Scary

Published July 2025 · 5 min read

Cross Join's may not always be the go-to,espeically when inner joins and left joins do the job so appropriately, but a cross join is great for finding those potential joins you might have missed.

What is a CROSS JOIN?

A CROSS JOIN returns every possible combination of rows between two tables — it's also known as a Cartesian Join.
It's like 5 kids in a candy store. kid_in_candy_store.jpg It joins all the kids with every candy in the store. So, if there's:
5 kids
10 candies
5X10 = 50. Cross join will show all 50 different combinations. 

SELECT *
FROM shirts
CROSS JOIN shoes;

This query will pair each shirt with every shoe. So if you have 5 shirts and 10 shoes, you’ll get 50 rows.

When Would You Use It?

Most people avoid CROSS JOINs, but they have their uses:

  • Generating test datasets
  • Creating scheduling combinations
  • Exploring all possible pairings

Common Pitfalls

Accidentally creating a CROSS JOIN is easy if you forget your ON clause when writing a regular JOIN. Be cautious!

Final Thoughts

CROSS JOINs are powerful, but like most tools, they work best when used intentionally. Keep experimenting and always check your row counts!
