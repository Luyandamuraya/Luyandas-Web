Data Analysis is a fundemental pillar to building the correct insights, but, if done incorrectly it can be catastrophic.
These are some of my lesson's on data anaysis.

Lesson 1: Stakeholder Requirments

Often, a project will have multiple stakeholders and lost in the mass of people, and meetings can become the project requirments. What you may have thought was a simple data extraction, could have intended to be a data dashboard but this request was lost in the stream of people.

Think of 5 kids in a candy store.


Each Kid has the possibility of taking each candy - it shows us this. It joins all the kids with every candy in the store. So, if there's:

  • 5 kids

  • 10 Candies


    • 5X10 = 50. Cross join will show us allb> 50 different combinations. 

    SELECT *
FROM candy
CROSS JOIN kids;

    This query will pair each candy with every kid.

    When Would You Use It?

    Most people avoid CROSS JOINs, but they have their uses:

    • Generating test datasets
    • Creating scheduling combinations
    • Exploring all possible pairings

    Common Pitfalls

    Accidentally creating a CROSS JOIN is easy if you forget your ON clause when writing a regular JOIN. Be cautious!

    Final Thoughts

    CROSS JOINSs are powerful,and should only be used when testing or exploring all pairings. Keep experimenting and always check your row counts!
